Our Provenance

The Geometry of Heritage

Every thread tells a story of an ancestor's hands. We collaborate with master artisans in four historic clusters across India to revive techniques that have weathered centuries.

location_on KUTCH, GUJARAT

Ajrakh

Derived from the Arabic word 'Azrak' meaning blue, Ajrakh is a complex 16-step block-printing process using natural indigo, madder, and pomegranate. It mirrors the star-speckled desert sky through cosmic geometric patterns.

Bagru

Famous for its characteristic 'Seyali' (black) and 'Dabu' (resist) prints, Bagru is defined by its use of 'Syahi'—a paste made from fermented iron filings and jaggery—to create deep, earthy motifs that resonate with the Rajasthani soil.

location_on CHITTORGARH, RAJASTHAN

Dabu

The mud-resist technique, or Dabu, involves applying a mixture of black clay, sawdust, and gum to the fabric before dyeing. This protective layer ensures parts of the fabric remain untouched, creating sharp, ivory-toned silhouettes against deep dye.

Shop Dabu pieces arrow_forward
location_on DHAR, MADHYA PRADESH

Bagh

Named after the Bagh river whose mineral-rich waters enhance the vibrancy of the red dyes, this craft uses goat droppings and oil in its pre-treatment, creating a depth of color that is impossible to replicate chemically.
